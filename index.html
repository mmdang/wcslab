About WCS Lab

Advancing wireless communication theory and systems

The Wireless Communications System Laboratory (WCS Lab) conducts research on next-generation wireless communication systems, with emphasis on physical layer security, MIMO technologies, reconfigurable intelligent surfaces, relay networks, and V2X communications.

Our work combines mathematical performance analysis, information-theoretic security, and practical system insights to develop secure and intelligent communication technologies for future wireless networks.

Research Areas

Core research topics

Physical Layer Security

Secrecy outage probability, secrecy capacity, eavesdropping models, and secure transmission in fading channels.

RIS-assisted Communications

Performance analysis and secure transmission using reconfigurable intelligent surfaces under practical constraints.

MIMO Systems

Beamforming, antenna selection, user scheduling, diversity combining, and multi-user MIMO performance analysis.

Cooperative Relay Networks

AF/DF relaying, relay-user selection, dual-hop systems, and diversity schemes over Nakagami and Rayleigh fading.

V2X Communications

Secure and reliable wireless communication technologies for vehicle-to-everything systems.

AI-enabled Wireless Systems

Emerging research on intelligent communication systems and AI-assisted wireless network design.
